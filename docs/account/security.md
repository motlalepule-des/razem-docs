## Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Razem requires 2FA for all payment confirmations and sensitive account changes. Supported methods:

| Method | Security Level | Setup |
|:---|:---|:---|
| SMS OTP | Medium | Default (set up at registration) |
| Authenticator App | High | Settings → Security → 2FA |
| Biometric (mobile app) | High | Available in Razem mobile app |

### Setting Up an Authenticator App

1. Download an authenticator app (e.g., Google Authenticator, Authy)
2. Go to **Settings → Security → Two-Factor Authentication**
3. Click **Add Authenticator App**
4. Scan the QR code with your app
5. Enter the 6-digit code to verify
6. Save your backup codes in a secure location

{: .warning }
Store your backup codes safely. If you lose access to your authenticator and have no backup codes, account recovery may take up to 5 business days.

## Password Security

### Password Requirements

- Minimum 12 characters
- At least one uppercase letter
- At least one number
- At least one special character (`!@#$%^&*`)
- Cannot be a previously used password (last 10)

### Changing Your Password

1. Go to **Settings → Security → Password**
2. Enter your current password
3. Enter and confirm your new password
4. Verify with 2FA

### Forgot Password

1. Click **Forgot Password** on the login page
2. Enter your registered email address
3. Check your email for a reset link (valid for 1 hour)
4. Create a new password

## Device Management

Manage devices that have access to your account:

1. Go to **Settings → Security → Devices**
2. View all active sessions with device info and location
3. Click **Revoke** next to any unrecognized device

### Trusted Devices

Mark a device as trusted to reduce 2FA prompts:

1. Log in from the device
2. When prompted for 2FA, check **Remember this device for 30 days**

## Login Notifications

Receive alerts for:

- New device logins
- Logins from new locations
- Failed login attempts
- Password changes

Configure in **Settings → Notifications → Security Alerts**.

---

## Account Freeze

If you suspect unauthorized activity:

1. Immediately freeze your account via **Settings → Security → Freeze Account**
2. Or call our 24/7 security line: **+27 800 RAZEM 1**
3. A frozen account cannot make payments until unfrozen by you or Razem support

---

## Security Best Practices

- Never share your password or OTP with anyone — Razem staff will never ask for these
- Use a unique password not used on other sites
- Log out after each session on shared devices
- Enable login notifications
- Regularly review transaction history for unauthorized activity
- Keep your contact details up to date for OTP delivery
